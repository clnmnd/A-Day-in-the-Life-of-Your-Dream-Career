Morning Routine

I start my day early, usually around 6:00 AM. After getting dressed in my scrubs and grabbing a quick breakfast, I head to the hospital or clinic. The morning begins with reviewing my schedule and patient charts. I meet with patients who are pregnant, checking on their health and the baby’s development. I listen to the baby’s heartbeat and answer any questions the mothers have. Sometimes, I help women with other health concerns like menstrual issues or hormonal changes. I find the mornings very rewarding because I get to support and care for women during important times in their lives.

Midday Tasks & Projects

By midday, I often perform prenatal ultrasounds or assist with deliveries if it's a busy day at the hospital. I work closely with nurses, midwives, and other doctors to make sure everything goes smoothly. I also see patients for annual exams and perform minor surgeries when needed. There’s a lot of talking and explaining, making sure my patients understand their options and feel comfortable. Paperwork and updating records take up part of my day, but that’s important to keep track of each patient’s care plan.

A Story from My Dream Job

One of my most unforgettable days was when I helped a woman through a difficult delivery. She was having her first baby and was very nervous. During labor, there were some complications, and I had to act quickly to make sure both mother and baby were safe. After several intense hours, the baby was born healthy and strong. The relief and joy in the mother’s eyes made all the hard work worth it. Later, when she held her baby for the first time, I felt proud to be part of such a special moment in their lives. It reminded me why I love being an OB-GYN — helping bring new life into the world and supporting women’s health.
